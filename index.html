What nobody tells you

Korea's surgeons are extraordinary. The system around them wasn't built for you.

We believe you deserve the truth before you fly eleven hours. These are the four structural problems every international patient faces — reported by CNN, Al Jazeera, and The Korea Herald, not by us.

No. 01

The clinic's "coordinator" is a sales role

The friendly English speaker at most clinics is employed to book procedures. Fluent on the upsell, vague on the risks — and your nuanced wishes can be compressed into a single translated sentence. Informed consent requires a voice that answers only to you.

No. 02

Hidden commissions inflate your quote

Illegal brokers routinely add 30–50% — in documented cases up to 90% — on top of what Korean patients pay for the identical procedure. Taxi drivers, hotel staff, and Instagram "consultants" all collect referral fees. If your helper is paid by the clinic, you are the product.

No. 03

"Ghost surgery" is why the CCTV law exists

Korea legislated operating-room CCTV in 2023 precisely because surgeon substitution happened at scale. Patients now have the legal right to request recording under general anesthesia — but you must ask in writing, before surgery, and most foreigners are never told.

No. 04

After the operation, you're on your own

The surgery takes hours. The recovery takes weeks — in a city where you can't read a pharmacy label, alone in a hotel room. And once you fly home, many patients describe the same thing: silence.

None of this means you shouldn't come.
It means you shouldn't come alone.

Our lines in the sand

You choose your surgeon; we equip you to choose well — with Korea's public registries (KSPRS board certification, KAHF accreditation, the Medical Korea portal) and the questions that matter. A recommendation can be bought. A framework can't.

Never take a commission.

Not a fee, not a kickback, not a "marketing partnership," not a discount passed to us instead of you. One client, one flat fee, full stop.

Never book in your name.

Your clinic, your flights, your hotel — you book them all directly, in your own name, at direct prices. We are your interpreter and care companion, not a travel agency or a patient-referral business. It keeps your money clean and our loyalty undivided.

Never give medical advice.

We are not doctors and won't pretend to be. Every medical decision belongs to you and the licensed physicians you choose. Our job is to make sure nothing is lost between you.

Never rush you.

"The price expires Friday" is a sales tactic, not medicine. If anyone pressures you to decide quickly, we'll be the voice in your ear saying: you're allowed to sleep on it.

Never speak of your visit.

Discretion is absolute. We're happy to sign an NDA, and many clients' families, employers, and followers simply know they took a trip to Korea.

Questions, answered honestly

Frequently asked

Are you a medical tourism agency or broker?

No — and the difference is the entire point. Agencies and brokers are typically paid by clinics for delivering patients, which is why their services are "free to you." We work on the opposite model: you pay us a flat fee directly, we accept nothing from any clinic, and we never recommend or introduce clinics. We are interpreters, coordinators, and advocates — hired by you, answerable to you.

If you won't recommend a clinic, how do I choose one?

Better than most patients do — because you'll be using the same public tools informed Koreans use. We give every client a research framework: the KSPRS registry to verify board-certified plastic surgeons, KAHF accreditation for hospitals serving international patients, the government's Medical Korea portal, and a guide to reading past marketing. You shortlist; when you consult, we're in the room making sure you hear everything — including what the clinic's own coordinator might soften.

Why so strict? Because the moment a service profits from your choice of clinic, its advice is worth nothing. We'd rather be genuinely useful than pleasantly compromised.

Do you arrange my hotel, transport, or clinic appointments?

No — deliberately. You book your clinic, your flights, and your hotel yourself, directly and in your own name, at direct prices. What we provide is what you can't book online: a certified, native-level English interpreter and care companion beside you at every step. We'll advise on timing — how long your procedure really needs — and we'll be with you everywhere, but the bookings, and the prices, stay yours.

What do your services cost?

A flat fee, quoted in writing after your free video call, based on your dates and the level of care you want — never a percentage of your surgery, never adjusted by what you spend at the clinic. Most clients find our fee is a small fraction of what hidden broker commissions would have silently added to their quote.

Can we talk before I commit to anything?

Please do. Every engagement starts with a free 30-minute video call — WhatsApp, FaceTime, or Zoom, at a time that suits your time zone. We'll talk through your procedure, dates, and worries. If we're not the right fit, we'll say so.

I'm coming alone. Is that... okay?

Most of our clients come alone — it's precisely why we exist. From the arrivals hall to your flight home, you'll have one consistent, English-speaking person beside you: at consultations, on treatment morning, when you wake up, and through the quiet recovery days when being alone in a foreign city feels heaviest. Solo female travelers often tell us that having a woman alongside throughout was what made the trip feel possible.

What happens if something goes wrong?

We can't perform medicine and we won't promise outcomes — no honest person can. What we can promise: you will never face a problem through a language barrier. If a complication arises, we're with you at the clinic immediately, interpreting precisely, documenting everything in English, and making sure your concerns are heard as loudly in Korean as you'd voice them at home. After you leave Korea, we remain your bridge to the clinic rather than letting the conversation go silent.

How private is this?

Completely. We share nothing with anyone — no testimonials without written consent, no photos, no "success stories" scraped from your trip. We'll sign an NDA on request. Discretion isn't a premium add-on; it's the default.
