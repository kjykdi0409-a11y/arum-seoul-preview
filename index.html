Two journeys

Both deserve someone who speaks both languages — the medical one, and the one the city is actually lived in.

Journey One

Skin & Seoul

Dermatology, and the week around it

Korea's dermatology is genuinely a decade ahead — lasers, boosters, lifting, treatments that simply aren't offered at home yet, at prices that make the flight pay for itself. But the good clinics don't advertise in English, and the ones that do aren't always the good ones.

We talk it through before you fly, agree on a shortlist together, and book it. Then we're with you at the appointments — translating what the doctor actually said, not the sales version.

And because skin treatments leave you free by the afternoon, the rest of the week is yours: the food you've been reading about, the palaces, the pottery village two hours out, the Olive Young run where someone tells you which of the four hundred products is worth it.

Right for you if — you want lasers, injectables, boosters, lifting or a full skin reset, and you'd like the trip to feel like a holiday rather than a hospital stay.
Journey Two

Surgery & Recovery

The week that asks the most of you

Surgery abroad is a different kind of week. There's a consultation where every word matters, a morning you'd rather not face alone, and days afterward when you're swollen, tired, and eight thousand kilometres from anyone who knows you.

We're there for all of it. In the consultation room, interpreting the risks as carefully as the promises — and making sure the surgeon who consults you is named, in writing, as the one who operates. On the morning itself. And in the days after, when the swelling peaks and someone needs to bring the arnica, the cold compress, the soft food, the hat you can hide under.

Those supplies are bought and waiting before you land. That's the part nobody tells you about, and the part we take most seriously.

Right for you if — you're having a surgical procedure and you want someone in the room, on the day, and through the recovery.

Whichever week you're here for — the city comes included.

Why any of this matters

Korea's clinics are extraordinary. The system around them wasn't built for you.

We'd rather you heard this from us than discovered it at the counter. All of it has been reported by CNN, Al Jazeera and The Korea Herald — not by us.

No. 01

The clinic's "coordinator" is a sales role

The friendly English speaker at most clinics is employed to book procedures — fluent on the upsell, vague on the risks. Your careful description of what you want can arrive as one flat sentence. Informed consent needs a voice that answers only to you.

No. 02

Hidden commissions inflate your quote

Brokers routinely add 30–50% — in documented cases up to 90% — on top of what Korean patients pay for the same procedure. Taxi drivers, hotel staff and Instagram "consultants" all collect referral fees. When your helper is paid by the clinic, you are the product.

No. 03

The CCTV law exists for a reason

Korea legislated operating-room CCTV in 2023 because surgeon substitution happened at scale. You have the right to request recording under general anaesthesia — but you must ask in writing, before surgery, and almost no foreign patient is told.

No. 04

Afterwards, most people are on their own

The procedure takes an hour. The week takes a week — in a city where you can't read a pharmacy label, in a hotel room, with a face you don't recognise yet. This is the part we were built for.

How five to seven days actually go

Day one — Arrival

Then we meet at your hotel: the plan for the week, your questions, where the nearest pharmacy and convenience store are, and a first meal somewhere good.

The middle — Treatment

The days that matter, accompanied from morning

Consultation, treatment, follow-up — we're with you from the start of the day. Interpreting, watching the paperwork, asking the questions you shouldn't have to know to ask. If it's surgery, we're there before you go in and there when you come round.

Afterwards, back to the hotel, with the supplies already in the room.

Between — Yours

The days we're not together are still covered

Three of your days include full accompaniment on a five-to-seven-day trip; four on a longer one. The others, Seoul is yours — with plans already made, reservations already booked, and one message away if anything at all comes up. Lost, confused, unwell, or just want company for dinner: message.

And if recovery turns harder than expected, we don't watch the clock. Staying longer because you need us is our call to make, and it isn't billed. Extra days are for the good version — when you're well and want another day out in the city.

Going home — And after

We don't disappear at the airport

Before you fly, a final check that you're fit to travel and clear on aftercare. After you're home, we stay your bridge to the clinic — questions relayed, answers translated, photos passed on. The conversation doesn't go quiet just because you did.

Our lines in the sand

What we will never do

Never take a commission.

Not a referral fee, not a kickback, not a "marketing partnership", not a discount quietly passed to us instead of to you. Our entire income is the fee on your invoice. It is the reason everything else on this page can be true.

Never send you somewhere because we're paid to.

We research clinics against your case and tell you what we found — including what we didn't like. You decide; we book what you chose. Since no clinic can pay us, no clinic can buy its way onto your shortlist.

Never mark up what you pay.

Your hotel costs what the hotel charges. Your treatment costs what the clinic charges. We don't take a slice of your bookings, and we'll show you the rates.

Never give medical advice.

We're not doctors and won't pretend to be. Every medical decision belongs to you and the licensed physicians you choose. Our job is making sure nothing is lost between you.

Never leave while you still need us.

Your package includes a set number of accompanied days. But if we judge that you need us longer — a hard recovery, a complication, a day that simply went wrong — we stay, and we don't charge you for it. Added days are for when you want more of Seoul, not for when you're struggling.

Never rush you.

"This price expires Friday" is a sales tactic, not medicine. If anyone pressures you, we'll be the voice saying: you're allowed to sleep on it, and you're allowed to walk out.

Never speak of your visit.

Discretion is absolute. No testimonials without your written consent, no photos, no stories. We'll sign an NDA on request.

Answered honestly

Frequently asked

Everyone else is free. Why do you charge?

Because "free" isn't free — it's just billed to you somewhere you can't see it. Agencies that cost you nothing are paid by clinics for delivering patients, and that money comes out of your quote. Documented broker commissions in Seoul have run from 30% to 90% of the surgery price.

So the real question isn't free versus paid. It's whether you'd rather pay a fee you can see, to someone who works for you, or a commission you can't see, to someone who works for the clinic. Ours is roughly what a US patient advocate charges for a single day — $150 to $325 an hour is the going rate there — for a week of care here.

When and how do I pay?

Two payments, to us. A $100 deposit when you decide to go ahead, which holds your dates and starts the clinic research — and the balance in person in Seoul, in cash, once you've arrived. The deposit comes off your total, so if you come it costs you nothing extra. If you cancel, we keep it: by then the research is done and we've turned other clients away to keep your week free.

The balance is in US dollars only, so please bring cash with you — Korean ATMs give out won, not dollars, and changing money on arrival is an errand you won't want on day one.

Everything else you pay directly to whoever provides it: the clinic, the hotel, the airline, the restaurant. We'll tell you exactly which hotel and why, and send you the booking — but you confirm it on your own card, at their price. We take no commission and add no markup, and the simplest guarantee of that is that your money never touches our account.

Do you choose the clinic for me?

No — we research it with you. We'll look properly into clinics suited to your case, check board certification through the Korean registries, and send you a shortlist with honest reasoning, including the reservations we have. You choose. Then we handle the booking and the scheduling.

The important part: because no clinic can pay us anything, nothing can buy its way onto that shortlist. That's what makes the recommendation worth having.

Why isn't airport pickup included?

Honestly? Because it would raise everyone's price for something most people don't need. Incheon to central Seoul is one of the easiest airport journeys in the world — the express train and the airport buses are excellent, and we'll send you exact, step-by-step instructions for your terminal, your hotel, and your arrival time.

If you'd still prefer to be met, we'll arrange a private transfer at cost. We just won't pad the package with it.

What does "three days of accompaniment" actually mean?

On three of your days, we're with you from the morning — consultations, treatment, follow-ups, and whatever else that day holds. Those are the days that need a person, not a phone.

On the other days, the planning is already done: your reservations are made, your itinerary is in your hand, and we're one message away. If something goes sideways — you feel unwell, you're lost, a clinic calls — we respond.

And if it turns out you need us for longer than the package covers, we stay. That judgement is ours to make and we don't bill you for it. Added days are for the opposite case: when you're doing well and simply want more of the city.

I'm coming alone. Is that all right?

Most of our clients do, and it's precisely why we exist. From your first day to your last, there's one consistent English-speaking person who knows your case, your plan, and your worries: at the clinic, on the difficult morning, and on the quiet afternoons when being alone in a foreign city feels heaviest. Solo travellers often say having a woman alongside is what made the trip feel possible at all.

Can I do treatments and still see Seoul?

That's the whole idea behind the Skin & Seoul week. Most dermatological treatments leave you free within hours, so the afternoons and the days between are genuinely yours — and we'll have planned them if you want, from the museums to the pottery village to the meal you'll still be thinking about next year.

Surgery is different, and we'll be straight with you about that: the days after are for resting. But even then there are gentle afternoons, and we'd rather plan those than pretend they don't exist.

What if something goes wrong?

We can't practise medicine and we won't promise outcomes — no honest person can. What we can promise: you will never face a problem through a language barrier. If a complication arises, we're at the clinic with you immediately, interpreting precisely, documenting everything in English, and making sure your concerns land in Korean as forcefully as you'd say them at home. Emergencies are answered at any hour, at no charge. And after you leave, we stay your bridge to the clinic rather than letting the conversation go silent.

How private is this?

Completely. Nothing is shared with anyone — no testimonials without written consent, no photographs, no stories. We'll sign an NDA on request. Discretion isn't a premium extra here; it's simply how we work.
